The Mix-in Master Protocol

Adding inclusions disrupts the gluten network and alters hydration. Don't guess. Level up your skills systematically from simple flours to complex architectural chunks.

The Apprentice

Target: Hydration & Texture

Lvl 1

The Apprentice

Alternate Flours

Low Risk

Powders integrate homogeneously without tearing gluten. Begin by replacing 10-20% of your bread flour with alternative grains.

  • Whole Wheat Flour
  • Rye Flour (Malty taste)
  • Semolina or Cocoa Powder

The Alchemist

Hydration Swaps

Medium Risk

Instead of using water, substitute your hydration with flavorful liquids. Monitor fermentation speeds carefully, as sugars interact dynamically.

  • Dark Beer or Stout
  • Buttermilk & Coffee
  • Fruit/Vegetable Juices

The Artisan

Seeds & Spices

Moderate Risk

Small bits distribute evenly but will steal hydration from your dough. Rule: Always boil/soak hard seeds for 10 minutes before integration.

  • Sesame & Chia Sequences
  • Cacao Nibs & Nuts
  • Marjoram, Thyme, Zests

The Chromatic

Visual Mastery

Advanced Risk

Mastering dyes requires tight hydration control. Powders dry the dough; juices liquefy it. Balance inputs meticulously.

  • Activated Charcoal (Black)
  • Butterfly Pea Powder (Blue)
  • Squid Ink & Beetroot

The Grandmaster

Heavy Chunks

Extreme Risk

Large obstacles physically sever the gluten network, killing oven spring. Must execute lamination integration. Monitor for melting cheese causing structural collapse or charring vegetables.

  • Chocolate Chunks & Drops
  • Heavy Cheeses (Cheddar, Feta)
  • Wet Olives & Black Garlic

👋 Hi, I’m Tjard — and this is Breadagain.

Three years ago, a colleague handed me a living, bubbling jar of wild yeast. It was a sourdough starter born in 2016, named Black Death (yes, really). I had no idea what I was doing — but I was instantly hooked.

My first bake was Joe Semola's "Easy Peasy Sourdough Bread." It wasn't perfect, but it was mine. That feeling of creating something real — with my hands, from simple ingredients — changed everything for me. I call it BIY: Bake It Yourself. Sourdough became my way to slow down, create, experiment, and have fun — even when the loaves turned out weird.

🍞 My Style

I love working with a mix of wheat, rye, and spelt, typically around 75% hydration — and yes, I still remember my first game-changer: chocolate sourdough. That was the moment it really clicked. I'm not a trained baker — just a home baker figuring things out as I go.

📒 Why I Made This Planner

When I started, I really wished something like this existed. A place to track my starter, understand hydration, avoid overproofed disasters, and repeat successful bakes.

So I created it — for beginners, for home bakers, and for anyone trying to figure it out loaf by loaf. This planner comes from experience — not perfection. Use it however you like, adapt it to your rhythm, and most importantly: enjoy the process.
